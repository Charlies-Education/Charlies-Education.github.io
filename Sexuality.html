Gay

Gay is a term generally used to describe when a man or man-aligned* person is attracted specifically to another man or man-aligned person, but it is also used as an umbrella term for anyones attraction to the same gender.

This flag is the mlm gay flag, but there is also the rainbow which is more widely known.


Lesbian

Lesbian is a term for when a woman or woman-aligned* person is attracted to another woman or woman aligned person.

This flag is the Sunset Lesbian flag, but there are many different versions. Not one is exactly right, so use whichever one you resonate with.


Bisexual and Pansexual

Bisexual and pansexual is the attraction to two or more genders. Within this community, there has been dispute on the difference between the two. I personally do not know the difference, but bisexuality is more categorized as the attraction to both male and female.

From top to bottom, it goes: Femininity, that attraction to both, and masculinity.


Like the Bi flag, it goes from top to bottom: Femininity, Nonbinary, and Masculinity.

Heterosexual

Heterosexual or "straight" is the romantic or sexual attraction to someone of the opposite gender.

This flag can also be seen as black and white stripes, but it in no way represents super straight, for it does not exist.

|->Heteroflexible



Homosexual

An outdated term which describes the romantic or sexual attraction to someone of the same gender. It is reccomded not to use this term.

An unbrella flag to represent same sex attraction.

|-> Homoflexible



Queer

Often used as a slur historically, Queer is an umbrella term in which any/all LGBTQ+ may identity. When refering to your sexuality specifically, it can mean tht you are anywhere on the sexuality spectrum except straight. It is most often used in this way by someone who is unsure of their sexuality.

There are a lot of versions of the queer flag, but the LGBTQIA+ Wiki explains it best.


Aromantic

Little to no romantic attraction to anyone of any gender.

The flag colors represent a wide aromantic spectrum, including Grey-Asexuality and platonic relationships.


Asexual

While being a spectrum, it is generally described as little to no sexual attraction to any gender.

Similar to the Aromantic flag, the colors represent a wide asexual spectrum. This includes community and Grey-Asexuals.

|->Greysexual, Greyromantic, Demiromantic, Demisexual



Gynesexual & Androsexual

These two identities are very similar to lesbian and gay, but there is a main difference; with Gynesexual, it is the attraction to anyone who presents femininity. Androsexual is the attraction to anyone who presents masculinity.

Top to bottom, it goes: Attraction to femininity, stability and support, and attraction to everyone


Top to bottom, it goes: Attraction to masculinity, stability and support, and attraction to everyone


Polyamorous

Polyamory most commomly reffers to someone who has attraction to multiple people at once, and engages in a relationship in which multiple people at the same time with their consent.

The flag colors represent love and passion, but also trust and value between partners.

*A woman or man aligned person is someone who is not entirely a woman or man, but feels more alined with that label. For example, a nonbinary person can still be a lesbian if they are woman-aligned and feel attraction mainly to women.
