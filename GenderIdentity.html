Cisgender

Cisgender, or cis, can be used to refer to anyone who identifies as the gender they were born with.

The Cisgender flag is not really a common or "official flag", but nonetheless it was made. I wouldnt reccomond using it, but you can.


Intersex

Intersex is a term used to describe someone who was born with the sex characteristics of neither male or female.

The Intersex flag is has two non-gender related colors, and a circle used to represent being a whole.


Transgender

Transgender, or trans, is a term used to refer to someone whos gender identity doesnt correspond with their gender assigned at birth.

The pink is for baby girls, the blue is for baby boys, and the white is meant to represent transitioning.

(There are also many other flags, but a more "Specific generalized flag" would be the transmasc and transfeminine flags.)



Genderfluid

Genderfluid is a term that refers to someone whos gender changes over time.

From top to bottom, it goes: Femininity, Lack of gender, both masc and fem, nonbinary, and Masculinity


Agender

Someone who is Agender is genderless, which falls under the nonbinary umbrella. Since it is described as a lack of gender, some people do not like using the term gender at all.

This flag generaly means a lack of gender and them mixed together. Hense the colors.


Nonbinary

Someone who considers themselves out of the binary genders of male and female.

Similar to Agender, this flag represents all genders and a lack of gender at the same time.


Androgyne

Someone who is androgynous is someone who fits inbetween male and female. This is also a term that can be used on clothing or styles to be described as 'gender neutral'.

From left to right, it details femininity, a mixture, and masculinity.


Bigender

Someone who is bigender is someone who may feel either fluidity in both genders or have two distint gender identities, or both! This term is sililar to genderfluid and nonbinary genders.

The pinks represent femininity, the more midtone colors represent androgyny, and the blues represent masculinity.


Two Spirit

Two spirit is an identity exclusively used for Indigenous North Americans and is an umbrella term for gender, sexuality, and/or spirituality

From top to bottom on the rainbow, it goes; Life, healing, sunlight, nature, serenity, and spirit.


The Trans Umbrella

There are many, many different gender identities that could be labled under the trans umbrella. Technically, "trans" is just someone who does not consider themleves to be cis, so you could consider all identities besides cis and/or sometimes intersex to go under this umbrella. More commonly though, you can see a smaller, nonbinary umbrella to describe identities like agender or genderfluid.



Gender vs sex

Gender Identity and sex are very important to distinguish, especially if you want to be able to support someone whos gender identity is different from their born sex.



One of the biggest "arguments" that transphobes use is how someone will never be a real man or woman because of their genitailia or how "they'll never be able to have kids" as if thats what makes you a man or woman. Which, it doesnt. A cis woman may be infertile, but shes still a woman. A trans woman may not be able to get pregnant, but it does not make her any less of a woman.



Another argument they'll make is about gender reassignment surgery. "Why would you need a surgery if you're truely a man/woman?" Please understand that while this question can be just an inquisitive one, it has also been used against me and other people I know with malicious intent. The answer to this question, though, is that it makes us confident. It makes us feel more like ourselves, and it is done with no intent of other peoples perceptions.
